Communications

Good communication is a critical success factor for achieving the goals of this complex project. This section of the kick off packages describes how to handle internal  communications, communications with external entities, communications with vendors and suppliers, and the use of  KUONI's Year2000 Web Site, http://www.kuoni.ch/y2k

Internal Communications

The intent of the Programme Office is to encourage open communications between organisations and the Programme Office. As special issues arise, escalation may be necessary to obtain approval. Project members should escalate through the project structure; team member to team leader, to local Y2K Project Manager, to Y2K Programme Office Manager (Rene BUCHLE), to Y2K Programme Manger (Koni ITEN) to executive Group Management (Max KATZ). Project members should not delay in escalating issues. An issue should be brought to the attention of the proper manager immediately. If your immediate supervisor is not available, escalate to the next level of the project or programme structure rather than wait for an individual to return.

Communications should be maintained openly between all levels of the structure. Appropriate reporting channels should be followed so personnel are aware of what is happening on the project, what design decisions are being made, etc. Key communications should be documented and kept as part of the project record.

All documentation (specifications, memorandum, e-mails, etc.) produced by the project will be maintained in a project notebook for review by project members. The notebook will be in the possession of the local Y2K Project Manger.

External Communications

External communication means any communications with an entity not part of the KUONI group.

Since every statement and even non statements may trigger serious legal consequences,  you have to note that

For the moment being:

For every wording to want to include in brochures and contracts and for every statement or mere information provided to external entities you are required to contact the Programme Office  for a review and approval first.

For the very  moment (April 1998) the KUONI's communication department  is working on a global communication concept which will define standardized wording and behavior for correspondence with all our suppliers and vendors to cover legal issues best and to streamline and co-ordinate the effort.

Please not that the only persons allowed to give official statements about readiness, budget and the status of the Y2K projects and programme are, on your request:  Koni ITEN and all members of the KUONI Group Management.

To the extent possible, this type of communications should be followed-up and documented in writing. A copy of the written correspondence should be provided to the Y2K Project Office.

For Y2K Programme Office assigned staff, all external communications will be reviewed by the Y2K Programme Office Manager. All external communications will be sent under official KUONI Group letterhead.

Vendor Communications

Special emphasis should be given to communications with vendors. In almost all cases, this type of communications should be documented in writing. Should the communications involve contract terms and conditions, changes to or amendments to an existing contract, then legal and or procurement review of written communications should be obtained prior to being released to the vendor. A copy of the written communications should be provided to the Y2K Programme Office.

www.kuoni.ch/y2k

The KUONI Year2000 Web Site, www.kuoni.ch/y2k, will serve as the principal electronic medium for disseminating information within and throughout the company. The URL of the KUONI's Y2K web site is http://www.kuoni.ch/y2k. The Programme Office are the entities responsible for managing this site.

The Y2K Programme Office may consider publishing a Y2K Newsletter as an alternate means of informing interested parties of the organisations Y2K efforts and activities.

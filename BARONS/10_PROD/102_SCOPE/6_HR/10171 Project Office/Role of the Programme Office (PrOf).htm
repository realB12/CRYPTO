Scope

The Y2K Programme Office operates on group level.

Our scope is purely technical and we care for Y2K compliance of

  • electrical devices owned or used by the Kuoni Group
  • electronical data received from or sent to our business partners or clients
  • external services and systems that exchange electronical data with Kuoni systems

Our job is co-ordination, controlling and guidance.

Therefore it remains the local organisation's responsibility to reduce business risks to a commonly agreed acceptance level. Also it is their responsibility to provide all needed resources.

Permanent Function and Roles

Since November 1997 the Y2K Programme Office is full time to:

  1. discuss issues, set direction and determine the status of the technical Y2K efforts undertaken by the KUONI Group.
  2. support inter organisational communication to provide synergies for joint problem-solving and information sharing.
  3. make consulting services available through the existing experts (in a joint effort working with KPMG).
  4. supervise collection and archive of the organisation's documents and reports to prove progress and compliance
  5. provide consistent methodology as a baseline for monitoring progress and quality on group level.
  6. watch and share best practice and behaviour of key players in the industry.
  7. provide a helpdesk to mange and escalate first contacts (telephone or e-mail).
  8. maintain and publish the programmes knowledge via Intranet (inventory, status, reports)
  9. provide technical advice
  10. report to Y2K Programme Manager (Koni Iten)

Kick Off Phase (completed)

  1. Rise general awarness of key IT people
  2. Introduce KUONI's Y2K Methodology (--> hand out of  Y2K Kick Off Package)
  3. Make sure that the local Y2K project team knows all details to perform assessment.
  4. Make sure that item maps and itemlists meet the group wide standard for inventory.

Review Phase (running)

  1. Conduct timely project status meetings and participate in Y2K project reviews.
  2. Work with organisations local Y2K project manager to develop and review local project plans and make sure that they are inline with the methodology
  3. Coordinate inter-organisation issues.
  4. Estimate remaining risk and rise issues when appropriate.
  5. Monitor and report on progress of the projects to the Programme Manager and others as appropriate.
  6. Ensures that appropriate documentation is defined, completed and maintained.
  7. Coordinate global infrastructure and tools requirements.

Certification Phase  (ca. June 1999)

  1. Define acceptance criteria

  2. Perform final review

  3. Decide and communicate certification

Contingency Phase (ca. Oktober 1999)

  1. Advise local project leader and local business manager for emergency handling:

  2. problem recognition

  3. problem helpdesk

  4. trigger

  5. communication of business contingency plans

  6. how to go back to normal when technical systems are repaired

War Room (ca. December 1999)

  1. Analyse business impact of Y2K failures and assist management in emergency handling.

  2. Activate and supervise recovery procedures and contingency plans

Not covered areas

Cost Control

Budget and Cost Control is done by the Programme Manager (Koni Iten). Besides the Programme Office holds no copy of any expense reports nor invoices once they have been given to the accounting department. We trust that all can be found there, should it be required again.

Supply Chaines and Services

All business issues which are not directly associated to electrical devices and therefore are covered by teh Y2K business manager (i.e. airlines, hotels, banks, insurance etc.).

Resources

It is the organisation's responsibility to provide all resources required for Y2K effort.

# MemeCoins

> “Memecoins can hardly be an ‘investment’ rather than quick trades” for investors growing irrational due to collective mental and hormonal shifts. 

Von Meme-Coins wird gesprochen, wenn Kryptowährungen im Zusammenhang mit Memes, die im Internet kursieren, entwickelt wurden. Ein Beispiel dafür ist der Dodgecoin, dessen Logo ein Shiba Inu, eine beliebte japanische Hunderasse, ziert. Influencer:innen und sowie Communities können solchen – auf den ersten Blick nutzlosen – Kryptowährungen Aufwind geben. Daher haben auch sie das Potenzial, an Wert zu gewinnen. Memecoins appear to rely solely on community, emotion and the promise of wealth, where traders are drawn to the sense of camaraderie and the promise of rapid gains (unlike other crypto coins with complex technical explanations and mechanics).

This comes with new metrics such as how strong a project’s key opinion leader (KOL) game is, along with volume and chart performance where “Memecoin investors are looking for 24-hour or less returns, whereas a typical venture capital firm has a seven-year outlook as they have realized that most tokens marketed as providing technical solutions lack real intrinsic value and utility beyond speculative price movements.

Memecoins might also be fueled by a “very strong sentiment that is anti-venture capitalism and anti-private equity". The love for memes combined with the power of mimetics might have created a social and psychological phenomenon where people want to unite with like-minded people.

While such motivation seems superficial to some, the strength of a shared ideal within a community of investors is significant as illustred by the unprecedented phenomena of the AMC and GameStop stock runs. They rally the troops, bringing a young, vibrant and energetic community together around an ideal or philosophy that they can make a long-lasting impact.

So, we might assume, that the memecoin economy thrives on hype, driven by impulsive traders chasing a quick buck. This mindset fuels extreme volatility and rapid liquidation cascades when the success of memecoins primarely lies in their marketing and hype, comparing them to clothing trends that come and go in a blink (remindes me of the Vegetable-Swatch-hype).

I personally believe that “projects that are building castles in the clouds are doomed to revert to their true value of zero.”
